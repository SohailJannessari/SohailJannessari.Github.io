In Irans 2005 Presidential elections, the results were, more than anything else, unexpected. Ahmadinejad, then mayor of Tehran, was a marginal figure even a week before the elections, ranking last or penultimate in most polls. Then something changed. His victory shocked pundits and politicians alike, resulting in a lot of unfounded explanations for his victory. Barely any quantitative research has been done into this result. In my research I look at how it happened.

so, here we start with looking at the relationship between urban vote and Ahmadinejads votes in the first round of the elections. The variable urban is calculated on the basis of Irans 2006 national census and the numbers it reported for rural and urban residents of each county. Election results were published at the county-level(), and didnt get more detailed than that.
